CLAUDE.md Setup Questionnaire

Set Claude up to know who you are and how you work — before you type a thing.

Why this matters — please read

This is the single most important step in getting Claude to work well for you. Claude reads a file called CLAUDE.md at the start of every session: a permanent briefing that tells it who you are, what you work on, and how you like to work. You won't have to re-explain anything, ever.

The quality of this file directly determines the quality of everything Claude does for you. The fifteen minutes you spend here carefully will save you hours over the months ahead. Be specific, give real examples, and write as much as is useful — there's no penalty for detail.

How to set up your file — about two minutes, no technical steps

  1. Fill in every answer box below as fully as you can.
  2. Open Cowork (or Claude Code).
  3. Select everything from the "copy from here" marker to the end of the document, and paste it in.
  4. Claude creates your CLAUDE.md file automatically — and reads it at the start of every session from then on.

Instruction for Claude: Below are my answers to a setup questionnaire. Please create a CLAUDE.md file in my current working folder that captures who I am, what I work on, the files and folders I use, and how I like to work. Organise it clearly with sensible headings, and use my answers below to write it. Ignore any questions I left blank.

Section 1 — Your role

1.1 What is your job title?
1.2 In a few sentences, what does your day-to-day work actually involve? (e.g. reviewing leases, preparing investor reports, managing valuations, handling KYC)
1.3 What are the 2–3 tasks you spend the most time on each week?
1.4 What software and tools do you use regularly? (e.g. Excel, SharePoint, specific databases, email)

Section 2 — Files and folders

This helps Claude understand where your work lives so it can reference files correctly.

2.1 What is the main folder on your computer where you keep your work files?
2.2 Are there specific subfolders Claude should know about? (e.g. investor reports, contracts, valuations)
2.3 Do you work with any shared SharePoint folders? If so, which ones most often?

Section 3 — How you like to work

These preferences shape how Claude communicates with you.

3.1 Do you prefer written output concise and to-the-point, or detailed and thorough? Any other preferences?
3.2 Should Claude ask clarifying questions before starting, or attempt the task and let you correct it?
3.3 Any formatting preferences? (e.g. always use bullet points, avoid jargon, use Dutch for certain tasks, summary at the top)
3.4 Is there anything Claude should never do or always avoid in its responses?

Section 4 — Your expertise

This helps Claude pitch explanations at the right level.

4.1 How comfortable are you with Excel and data work? (basic / intermediate / advanced — and any specifics)
4.2 How comfortable are you with technology in general? Any tools or concepts you find tricky?
4.3 What languages do you work in? (e.g. Dutch for client communication, English for reports)

Section 5 — Recurring tasks and templates

If Claude knows your recurring work, it can help much faster.

5.1 Any documents you produce regularly that follow a standard format? (monthly reports, investor updates, lease summaries)
5.2 Any standard phrases, sign-offs, or terminology RCP uses that Claude should always apply?
5.3 Any tasks where you copy-paste from old documents or redo the same steps repeatedly? (Claude may be able to automate these)

Section 6 — Anything else

6.1 Anything about your role, working style, or preferences Claude should know that wasn't covered above?
